🍽️ Mississippi Pot Roast

A cozy, hands-off slow cooker recipe that turns a simple chuck roast into buttery, tangy, fall-apart deliciousness.

Mississippi Pot Roast Recipe

Serves 6–8 • Prep Time: 5 minutes • Cook Time: 8+ hours on Low

Ingredients

  • 2–3 lbs chuck roast
  • 1 or 2 packets ranch dressing mix
  • 1 packet au jus gravy mix
  • 6 tablespoons salted butter, sliced
  • 1/3 cup juice from jarred pepperoncini peppers
  • 6–8 whole pepperoncini peppers (or sliced, if preferred)

Instructions

  1. Place the chuck roast directly into the bottom of your crock pot.
  2. Sprinkle the ranch dressing mix and au jus gravy mix evenly over the top of the roast.
  3. Add the sliced butter and pepperoncini peppers, then pour in the pepperoncini juice.
  4. Place the lid on and cook on LOW for 8 hours or more, until very tender.
  5. Around the 6-hour mark, taste the juices and adjust seasoning if needed.
  6. Shred the roast once tender and mix it into the cooking juices.
  7. Serve warm over noodles, rice, or mashed potatoes — or stuff it into toasted rolls for sandwiches.

💡 Tip: Add an extra splash of pepperoncini juice for a bolder tang.
